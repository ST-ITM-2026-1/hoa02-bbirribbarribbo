Testing the Hypothesis

Analyzing the Correlation Between Fashion Style and Social Communication

A research project that statistically examined the real influence of fashion style on social interaction — including first impressions, sense of closeness, and belonging — among university students in their twenties

  • Data Collection

    Conducted a Google Forms survey with 71 university students (39 female, 32 male) and performed Z-tests and T-tests using R

  • Hypothesis Testing

    Three hypotheses were tested — examining whether belonging influences department jacket usage, whether humanities students are more fashion-conscious than STEM students, and whether women feel greater closeness than men when discovering similar styles. All three hypotheses were ultimately rejected, as no statistically significant differences were found in any case.
